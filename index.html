About

I am broadly interested in developing statistically principled and computationally efficient methods for analyzing heterogeneous and incomplete data, especially in biomedical and public health settings. My recent work focuses on integrating multi-center datasets under blockwise missing covariates, with an emphasis on robust prediction and fairness across populations.

Previously, I completed my undergraduate studies in financial engineering and my graduate training in biostatistics. I have experience collaborating with faculty in statistics and biostatistics on projects involving robust machine learning, causal inference, and longitudinal data analysis.

Research

My research interests include:

  • Robust estimation under blockwise missingness
  • Group distributionally robust optimization for multi-source data
  • Semi-supervised learning with auxiliary covariates
  • Fair and stable prediction in EHR and biomedical applications

I am currently working on methods that combine maximin optimization with blockwise missingness structures to provide worst-case performance guarantees across heterogeneous centers.
