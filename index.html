About

I am an M.S. student in Biostatistics at Columbia University Mailman School of Public Health. I am broadly interested in statistically principled and computationally efficient methods for analyzing heterogeneous and incomplete data, especially in biomedical and public health settings.

My background combines financial engineering and biostatistics, which has given me a strong foundation in probability, stochastic processes, optimization, and modern statistical methods. I enjoy working at the interface of theory and applications, particularly when real-world issues such as missingness, distribution shift, and fairness play a central role.

Ongoing Projects

Optimal Subsampling for Single-Index Models
Research project, Aug. 2025 – Present, Shenzhen, China
Advisor: Prof. Jiasheng Shi

  • Developing communication-efficient subsampling strategies for federated learning across multi-source datasets with high data transfer costs.
  • Tracing the evolution of optimal subsampling from linear models to GLMs and single-index models to guide principled design choices.
  • Extending optimal subsampling methodology from parametric models to semi-parametric single-index models while preserving statistical efficiency from an L-optimality perspective.
  • Leading implementation and manuscript preparation; the resulting paper is ready for journal submission.

Maximin Effect Estimation with Blockwise Missing Data in Multi-Source Studies
Research project, Jun. 2024 – Present, New York, USA
Advisor: Prof. Molei Liu

  • Developing robust methods for estimating linear models in a target population using multiple heterogeneous source datasets with blockwise missing covariates.
  • Introducing a maximin effect approach that maximizes the minimum explained variance across sources to ensure robustness under covariate shift.
  • Proposing doubly robust estimation strategies that incorporate density ratio estimation and cross-fitting to address covariate shift and model misspecification.
  • Conducting simulations and contributing to theoretical development; this methodological paper is also ready for submission.
